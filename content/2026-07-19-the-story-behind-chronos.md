If you’ve ever visited InSpace, you’ve probably wondered why we have a computer sitting on display. At first glance, it just looks like another desktop.
It isn’t.

> Behind that glass sits **Chronos**, a workstation with 96GB RAM, a 16 core Intel Core i9 processor, and a dedicated 16GB NVIDIA RTX 4070 Ti Super GPU.

If you’re hearing those specifications for the first time, they might sound like a mouthful. What they really mean is simple: **Chronos is built for serious creative and technical work**. In simpler terms, it is designed for tasks that ordinary computers struggle with, from AI development and 3D rendering to video production, game development and engineering simulations.

More importantly, _Chronos_ is the first major piece of equipment in what will eventually become the InSpace MakerSpace. Still to come are a 3D printer, a 3D recycler, a programmable robot dog, GPU expansion units, and other tools that will continue expanding what our community can build and create.

### Why We Acquired Chronos

_Chronos_ wasn’t purchased by one person due to it's cost (like buying 3 Macbook Pro laptops). It became a reality because several like minded people chose to contribute towards a shared vision. At InSpace, we believe collectivism is one of society’s most underrated strengths. It is the idea that people can accomplish far more together than they ever could individually.

"So, why did we acquire _Chronos_? The answer lies in one word: **Collectivism**."

One of the most fascinating illustrations of this is the story of the _Tower of Babel (Genesis 11:1–9)_. Whatever your interpretation of the story, one thing stands out: people working together with a shared purpose are capable of extraordinary things. That spirit of collective effort is one of the foundations on which InSpace is being built.

Our goal is to grow the Port Harcourt technology ecosystem by providing the infrastructure, systems and educational resources people need to build. We believe innovation requires more than ideas. It requires the right tools, practical skills and access to both software and hardware.

Everyone who wants to design products, train AI models, create animations, edit films, build games or develop engineering projects, should have access to tools that makes those ambitions possible. We want people in Port Harcourt to build solutions that solve real problems locally, nationally and, ultimately, globally.

The shift has already begun. Many people simply have not noticed because they have not been paying attention. At InSpace, we constantly ask ourselves **what we need to become limitless?**

We do not have all the answers yet, and we do not pretend to. What we do know is that every barrier we remove gives someone the freedom to build, create and solve bigger problems. We are committed to removing those barriers one by one. 

For many creators, the biggest barriers are not talent or ideas. They are reliable electricity, reliable internet and access to the computing power they need. Removing those barriers can tremendously change how we learn, work, grow and create as a community. We believe we have made significant progress on the first two. Now, we are addressing the third.

That brings us to the exciting part.

Starting **Friday, July 10**, Chronos will be open to members of the InSpace customers and community members on a strict booking basis. Reservations can be made for **Wednesdays and Fridays, between 9:00 a.m. and 8:00 p.m.**, and access is currently available to **InSpace Weekly and Monthly subscribers**.

If you’re wondering what you can actually do with _Chronos_, let me broaden your mind.

### Some ideas on what you can do
I learnt 3D modeling with _Blender_, but eventually lost interest because rendering took decades. Later, I tried learn _Adobe After Effects_, but my computer simply could not keep up. I am sure there is something you have always wanted to learn, build or explore, but your computer simply couldn’t keep up either. Hopefully, that is one excuse you no longer have to give.

We cannot wait to see what you create with _Chronos_, and we hope it brings you one step closer to your personal and professional goals.

> Every tool we add brings someone one step closer to building what once seemed impossible.
